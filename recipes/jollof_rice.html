Jollof Rice

Jollof rice is a vibrant and flavorful West African dish made with long-grain parboiled rice cooked in a rich tomato-based sauce. Its core ingredients typically include tomatoes, red bell peppers, onions, garlic, and a blend of spices like thyme, curry powder, and bay leaves. The dish is simmered until the rice absorbs the deeply spiced sauce, creating a one-pot meal that is smoky, slightly sweet, and savory. Depending on the region or personal preference, it can be made with vegetables, chicken, beef, fish, or left plain.

Jollof rice is more than just food—it's a cultural icon and a symbol of pride across West Africa, especially in Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal, each claiming to make the best version. It is often the centerpiece at parties, weddings, and festive gatherings, served with fried plantains, salads, or grilled meats. While its origins are traced back to the Senegambian region, Jollof rice has evolved into a beloved and fiercely debated dish, with each country adding its unique twist. Its bold flavor, color, and aroma make it an unforgettable experience in African cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Long-grain parboiled rice (preferably Nigerian or golden sella)
  • Tomatoes (fresh or canned)
  • Red bell peppers
  • Onions
  • Tomato paste
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Salt
  • Seasoning cubes or powder (e.g. Maggi or Knorr)
  • Thyme
  • Curry powder
  • Bay leaves
  • White or black pepper
  • Paprika or chili powder (optional, for extra heat)
  • Vegetable oil (or any cooking oil)
  • Chicken or beef stock (for more flavor)
  • Water (as needed)
  • Butter (for extra richness)
  • Smoked fish or dried prawns (for depth of flavor)
  • Mixed vegetables (e.g. carrots, peas)
  • Protein of choice – chicken, beef, goat meat, or sausages (pre-cooked)

    • Steps

    1. Blend the following until smooth: Fresh tomatoes Red bell peppers Onions Garlic and ginger.
    2. Pour the blended mixture into a pot and boil it (uncovered) until most of the water evaporates and it becomes a thick paste. This prevents soggy rice later.
    3. In a large pot, heat vegetable oil and sauté chopped onions until fragrant.
    4. Add tomato paste and fry it for a few minutes to reduce its sourness.
    5. Add the blended tomato mixture and cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it thickens and the oil begins to separate.
    6. Season with: Salt Thyme Curry powder Seasoning cubes or powder Bay leaves Paprika or chili powder (optional)
    7. Pour in chicken or beef stock (or water) and bring to a boil.
    8. Rinse the parboiled long-grain rice and add it to the sauce.
    9. Stir well so the rice is coated with the sauce. The liquid should just barely cover the rice.
    10. Cover the pot with foil or a tight lid to trap the steam.
    11. Reduce the heat to low and let it cook slowly for about 25–35 minutes.
    12. Check occasionally (without stirring too often) to avoid burning. If needed, add a small amount of water or stock and continue steaming.
    13. Once the rice is tender and the liquid is fully absorbed, fluff it gently with a fork.
    14. For extra flavor, you can stir in a knob of butter or mix in vegetables or protein.
    15. Serve hot with fried plantains, salad, chicken, beef, or any preferred side.
