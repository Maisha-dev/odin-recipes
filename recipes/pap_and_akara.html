Pap and Akara

Pap and akara is a classic Nigerian breakfast combination that pairs a smooth, creamy corn porridge with golden, crispy bean cakes. Pap—also called ogi—is made from fermented maize and cooked into a thick, custard-like consistency, often sweetened with sugar and enriched with milk. It has a slightly tangy flavor from the fermentation process and is enjoyed hot, making it both soothing and filling. Akara, on the other hand, are deep-fried fritters made from peeled black-eyed beans blended with onions, peppers, and seasoning. The mixture is fried until the outside is crisp while the inside stays soft and fluffy.

Together, pap and akara create a comforting balance of textures and flavors: the warm, silky pap contrasts perfectly with the crunchy, savory akara. This duo is not only popular for breakfast but also enjoyed as a light evening meal or street food snack across Nigeria and West Africa. Beyond its taste, it's a meal that carries cultural nostalgia—often associated with family mornings, bustling roadside stalls, and traditional weekend treats. It's simple, hearty, and deeply rooted in everyday Nigerian life.

Ingredients

  • Fermented maize (corn) paste or powder
  • Water (for mixing and cooking)
  • Sugar or honey (optional, for sweetness)
  • Milk (powdered, evaporated, or fresh — optional)
  • Black-eyed beans (peeled)
  • Onions (chopped)
  • Fresh pepper (scotch bonnet or chili)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Seasoning cube or powder (optional)
  • Water (for blending)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Steps

  1. Place the fermented maize paste (or powder) in a bowl. Add a little water and stir until it forms a smooth, lump-free mixture.
  2. Heat clean water in a kettle or pot until it reaches boiling point.
  3. Gradually pour the boiling water into the pap mixture while stirring continuously. Keep stirring until the pap thickens to your desired consistency.
  4. Soak black-eyed beans in water for a few minutes. Rub between your palms or blend briefly to remove the skins, then rinse until the skins are gone.
  5. In a blender, combine the peeled beans, chopped onions, and fresh peppers. Add just enough water to blend into a thick, smooth paste.
  6. Pour the paste into a bowl, add salt and seasoning cube/powder (optional), and mix well.
  7. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan. Scoop spoonfuls of the mixture into the hot oil, frying in batches. Fry until golden brown on all sides, then remove and drain on paper towels.
  8. Add sugar or honey (optional) and milk to the pap for extra creaminess. Serve hot, alongside akara.
